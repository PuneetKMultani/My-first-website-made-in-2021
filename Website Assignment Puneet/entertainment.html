    • The Golden Globes:

    Awarded by the 93 members of the Hollywood Foreign Press Association starting in January 1944, the Golden Globe Awards honour excellence in film, both American and International, and American television.

    The annual awards ceremony is usually held every January, and is a big part of the awards season for the film industry, culminating in the Academy Awards each year. The Golden Globes eligibility period refers to the calendar year (i.e., from January 1 to December 31). On January 5, 2020, the 77th Golden Globe Awards were held which honoured the best in film and television in 2019.Some of the winners of 2019 are the following:

    • The Grammys:

    A Grammy Award (stylized as GRAMMY, originally known as the Gramophone Award), or Grammy, is an award given by The Recording Academy to recognise music industry accomplishments. A golden gramophone is portrayed at the trophy. The annual presentation ceremony includes performances by famous musicians, and the presentation of more common interest awards. The Grammys are the second of the major music awards presented annually by the Big Three networks (between the fall American Music Awards and the summer Billboard Music Awards).

    As well as the Academy Awards (film), the Emmy Awards (television), and the Tony Awards (theatre and Broadway), it is considered one of the four major annual American entertainment awards.

    On May 4, 1959, the First Grammy Awards ceremony was held to recognise and respect the performers' musical accomplishments for the year 1958. The Academy overhauled several 2012 Grammy Award categories after the 2011 ceremony.The 62nd Annual Grammy Awards were presented at the Staples Center in Los Angeles on January 26, 2020.   • Song of the year- Bad Guy
  • Best new Artist- Bilie Eilish
  • Best pop solo performance- Truth Hurts (Lizzo)
  • Best pop duo- Old town road
  • Best dance recording- Got to keep on

    • The Oscars:

    The Academy Awards, more popularly known as the Oscars, are awarded to the film industry for their creative and technical merit. The awards, which are granted annually by the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences (AMPAS), are an international acknowledgment of excellence in cinematic accomplishments as measured by the voting membership of the Academy. A copy of a golden statuette, officially called the "Academy Award of Merit," is awarded to the various category winners, although more commonly referred to by its nickname, the "Oscar." The statuette depicts a rendered knight in style of Art Deco.

    The award was originally sculpted from a concept sketch by Cedric Gibbons, by George Stanley. AMPAS first presented it at a private dinner at the Hollywood Roosevelt Hotel hosted by Douglas Fairbanks in 1929, in what would become known as the 1st Academy Awards. The ceremony of the Academy Awards was first transmitted in 1930 by radio and was first televised in 1953.It is the world's oldest film awards ceremony and is now televised live worldwide.It is also the oldest of the four major annual American entertainment awards; its other three counterparts – the Emmy Awards for TV, the Tony Awards for Theatre, and the Grammy Awards for Music – are based on the Academy Awards.Some of the winners of 2019 are the following:

  • Actor in a leading role- Rami Malek
  • Actor in a supporting role- Mahershala Ali
  • Actress in a leading role: Olivia Colman
  • Actress in a supporting role- Regina King
  • Animated feature film- Spider Man:Into the spider verse
  • Cinematography- Roma
  • Costume Design- Black Panther

    • The MTV VMAs

    An MTV Video Music Award (commonly abbreviated as a VMA) is an award given by cable channel MTV to recognise the best in the video music medium. Originally conceived as an alternative to the Grammy Awards (in the video category), the annual MTV Video Music Awards ceremony was often called the "Super Bowl for Youth" ceremony, an acknowledgement of the ability of the VMA ceremony to draw millions of teenagers into 20-somethings each year. By 2001 the VMA had turned into a coveted trophy.

    The statue given to the winners is an astronaut on the moon, one of MTV's earliest portrayals, and was colloquially called a moonman. Nevertheless, MTV's president Chris McCarthy announced in 2017 that the statue will from then on be named a "Moon Man." The statue was designed by Manhattan Design – also creators of the original MTV logo – based on Fred Seibert 's 1981 "Top of the Hour" animation, created by Alan Goodman and created by Buzz Potamkin at Buzzco Associates. New York company, Society Awards, is now creating the statue.
